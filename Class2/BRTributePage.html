A Brief About Bob Ross

Bob Ross, full name Robert Norman Ross, was an American painter and art instructor who created and hosted the television program The Joy of Painting.

Ross was born in 1942 in Daytona Beach, Florida. His mother Ollie Ross was a waitress, and his father Jack Ross was a carpenter.

In 1961, he enlisted in the US Air Force at the age of 18. His highest rank was master sergeant as a clinic at Eielson Air Force Base in Alaska. During his military tenure, he developed an interest in paiting.

His show The Joy of Painting debuted in 1983 initially as an art class, but gained traction into a full series until 1984.

Ross died from lymphona on July 4th, 1995, at the age of 52.

Bob Ross has left a legacy carries on to modern day, mainly for his unique style of painting, his soft voice and personality, and his ASMR-esque presentations on his shows. He is even comparable to similar television personalities like Mister Rodgers or Bill Nye.
